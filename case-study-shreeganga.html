Understanding the Market

Ujjain isn't your typical hotel market. It's a pilgrimage city driven almost entirely by religious tourismâ€”specifically, visitors to the Mahakaleshwar Temple.

The challenge? Demand is incredibly seasonal. It spikes 500% during festivals like Mahashivratri and Shravan, then drops to near-zero during off-months. Most hotels in the area were treating this like a normal hospitality business, running static campaigns year-round.

Market analysis: Demand spikes 500% during religious festivals.

The "Pilgrim Family" Avatar

I identified the core avatar: The Devout Pilgrim Family. They aren't looking for luxury; they are looking for:

  • Safety (traveling with elderly parents/kids)
  • Vegetarian food (non-negotiable)
  • Proximity to temple (walking distance)

02

The "Pulse" Budget Strategy

Most hotels run the same budget every month. That's insane for a cyclical market.

I built a dynamic allocation model that syncs ad spend with the Hindu calendar.

Pulse Strategy: Aggressive spending only when demand exists.

The Split:

  • Off-Season (Maintenance): Minimal budget. Goal: Brand defense.
  • Peak Season (Aggressive): 3x Spend. Goal: 100% Impression Share during Mahashivratri.

Think of it like this: why waste money shouting when the streets are empty? Save it for when the crowd shows up.

03

The Campaign Structure: Google + Meta

Google Search: Capturing Intent

I avoided generic "hotels in Ujjain" keywords (too expensive, low intent). Instead, I built three focused campaigns:

  • 1. Branded Defense (â‚¹300/day): Stop OTAs (Goibibo, OYO) from hijacking our brand name.
  • 2. Proximity Intent (â‚¹1,500/day): Keywords like "Hotels near Mahakal Temple". These convert 3-4x higher.
  • 3. Emergency/Last Minute (Mobile): "Hotel available today". Captures spontaneous travelers already in the city.

Meta Ads: Creating Awareness

Audience: Interest stacking (Jyotirlinga, Hinduism, Spiritual Travel).

Creative: Vertical video showing the 5-minute walk from hotel to temple. Angle: "Stay where devotion meets convenience."

04

The Technical Setup

Leads aren't all equal. A 10-second call isn't a booking. I configured GTM to score lead quality:

  • Call Tracking: Calls > 60 seconds = "High Quality Conversion".
  • Form Scoring: Generic inquiries are filtered out; only forms with check-in dates count as conversions.

05

Ongoing Optimization

The "Weekend" Bid Adjustment

Data showed 49% of bookings happened Fri-Sun. I set automated rules to increase bids by 20% on weekends to capture planning traffic.

Location Radius Filtering

Locals were clicking ads but not booking rooms. I excluded Ujjain City (+5km radius) from hotel campaigns, saving budget for out-of-towners.

Search Term Sculpting

Aggressively negated terms like -jobs, -staff, -receptionist. We want guests, not employees.
